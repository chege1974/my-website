About Us

National Productivity and Competitiveness Centre (NPCC) is the country’s National Productivity Organization (NPO) responsible for spearheading the country’s Productivity Improvement Programmes (PIP) and enhancement of competitiveness in the public and private sectors across the national and county Governments. The Centre’s functions are anchored in the National Productivity Policy (Sessional Paper No. 3 of 2013) which was approved by the National Assembly on 4th August 2015 and Executive Order No. 1 of May 2016 on “Organization of the Government of the Republic of Kenya”.

In the exercise of its mandate, NPCC employs several productivity tools and techniques based on Kaizen/ Lean Philosophy in order to improve process efficiency and effective utilization of resources in the production of goods and services. The ultimate aim is to reduce the cost of doing business and enhance timely delivery of quality goods and services to the customers.

Vision


To be a centre of excellence for productivity management in Kenya


Mission


To promote best practices in the public and private sector and enhance factor productivity and competitiveness


Mandate of NPCC


NPCC’s mandate and functions are derived from the National Productivity Policy, Sessional Paper No. 3 of 2013 which also proposed the institutional and legal framework for the Centre’s operations. Its mandate entails promoting national Labour Productivity and Competitiveness.


Core Functions


1) Spearhead productivity movement in the country and ensure a paradigm shift to performance management.

2) Encourage and nurture the development of productivity culture in Kenya.

3) Promote the development and modelling of local industries to achieve best practices

4) Collect, collate, analyze and maintain a productivity data bank for dissemination of information from various sources

5) Provide a forum for promoting and sharing ideas and experiences on strategies, techniques and practices for productivity practices, accelerated economic growth and social development

6) Provide consultancy and training in productivity management

7) Promote productivity awareness in all sectors of the economy

8) Foster cooperation and collaboration between National Productivity Organizations (NPOs) in the African region

9) Assist private enterprises and public institutions in solving productivity related issues and problems
